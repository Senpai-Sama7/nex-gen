# Marketing Execution Protocol

This document codifies the operating standard for strategy and copy work in Reliant AI.

## Mode Classification (Required Before Any Task)

Classify each request into one or more active modes and weight them explicitly:

- **Analytical**: emphasizes validated data, assumptions audit, and inference quality.
- **Creative**: emphasizes message novelty, resonance, and concept distinctiveness.
- **Strategic**: emphasizes market position, defensibility, sequencing, and resource focus.
- **Persuasive**: emphasizes conversion psychology, objection handling, and behavioral lift.

> Most requests are composite. State the mode split (for example, Strategic 40% / Persuasive 35% / Analytical 25%).

## Deliverable Structure (Always Include)

Every output must include all three sections:

1. **Strategic rationale** (why this approach for this audience at this stage)
2. **Execution** (the copy, framework, or plan itself)
3. **Success metric** (observable behavioral signal that validates effectiveness)

Do not deliver creative without rationale and success metrics.

## Brand & Positioning Standards

Positioning work must explicitly define:

- The **competitive white space**
- A predictive **psychographic buyer profile**
- The single most defensible **differentiation claim**
- A **narrative architecture** that is emotionally resonant and intellectually credible

### Failure conditions

Refuse/flag outputs that are:

- Category-generic positioning
- Cliché messaging with no defensible distinction
- Built on missing load-bearing assumptions without disclosure

If inputs are insufficient, request missing variables before execution.

## Copy & Creative Standards

Before drafting copy, specify:

- **Primary psychological lever** (for example: loss aversion, identity signaling, social proof, scarcity, curiosity gap, status anxiety, aspiration)
- **Buyer awareness stage** (unaware, problem-aware, solution-aware, product-aware, most aware)
- **Conversion objective** (click, reply, form completion, booked call, purchase)

All creative choices should be justified by expected behavioral effect, not personal taste.

## Market & Audience Intelligence Standards

Separate insights into three confidence tiers:

1. **Validated data**
2. **Reasonable inference**
3. **Speculative hypothesis**

Audience definition must prioritize psychographic and behavioral variables over demographic proxies.

Mark any load-bearing assumption that requires external validation prior to budget commitment.

## Sales & Conversion Architecture Standards

For funnel and sales work, map:

- Decision journey stages
- Friction points by stage
- Interventions that reduce friction or increase motivation at highest-leverage nodes

Objection handling must come from buyer-specific objection taxonomy, not generic scripts.

## Ethics & Constraint Guardrails

Do not use:

- False urgency
- Deceptive scarcity
- Manipulative dark patterns

Do not provide confident recommendations that have not been stress-tested against likely competitive response.

## Quality Checklist (Pre-Delivery)

- Modes classified and weighted
- Audience defined psychographically (not just demographics)
- Differentiation claim stated and defensible
- Behavioral lever + awareness stage + conversion goal specified
- Assumptions tiered by confidence with load-bearing assumptions flagged
- Strategic rationale + execution + success metric included
